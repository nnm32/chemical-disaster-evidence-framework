# Chemical Disaster Evidence Framework

## Overview
The Chemical Disaster Evidence Framework is a structured decision-support prototype designed to organize environmental and health evidence following chemical incidents such as spills, fires, and derailments.

This tool focuses on identifying **evidence gaps**, not making claims of safety or risk.

---

## Purpose
Post-disaster response is often fragmented, with incomplete environmental sampling, limited biomonitoring, and unclear clinical follow-up pathways.

This framework aims to:
- Structure incident-level data collection
- Identify missing environmental and health evidence
- Support researchers, clinicians, and public health teams
- Promote transparent communication of uncertainty

---

## Core Features
- Incident intake module (location, exposure type, chemicals)
- Environmental sampling coverage tracking
- Automated evidence gap flagging
- Exportable structured summaries

---

## What This Tool Does NOT Do
- Does NOT determine safety of an environment
- Does NOT estimate individual health risk
- Does NOT replace toxicologists, environmental health experts, or public agencies

---

## Intended Users
- Environmental health researchers
- Public health teams
- Clinicians
- Disaster response analysts
- Students in preventive and environmental medicine

---

## Conceptual Basis
This framework is informed by observed gaps in real-world chemical disaster responses, including:
- Overreliance on targeted chemical testing
- Lack of non-targeted screening
- Absence of early biomonitoring
- Incomplete environmental sampling across domains
- Limited pathways for medical follow-up

---

## Technical Details
- Built with Streamlit
- Python-based
- Designed for rapid deployment and reproducibility
